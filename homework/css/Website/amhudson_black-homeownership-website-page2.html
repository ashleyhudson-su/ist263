Sundown Towns

Sundown Towns

Similarly, there were and still are, places called Sundown towns in the United States. These are towns that had signs that warned African Americans to leave the towns before the sunsets. These towns are notorious for their violence against black people to keep their communities all white. Martinsville Indiana is one of these towns.

In 1968, a young African American girl named Carol Jenkins was stabbed to death for selling encyclopedias after dark. Information from the 2019 census data revealed that black residents make up 1.3% of this city of over 11 thousand people.
