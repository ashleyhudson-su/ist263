De Jure Segregation

Governments Hand in Segregation

In the building of an integrated neighborhood in Milpitas California, the local government prevented Africa-Americans from using or building on land by declaring plots parklands. Not only that, roads that lead to African American communities were made private and then local government increased the rates for utilities.

Furthermore, local governments rerouted interstate highways to create boundaries or shift those that encamped African Americans neighborhoods. Equally as important, legislators selected specific school sites to force families to live in segregated neighborhoods to give their children an education.


Redlining

Neighborhoods with mostly black residents were redlined and lenders were five times more likely to give loans to white borrowers rather than black borrowers because of these redlined communities. Being in a redlined ditstict affected residents even if they had a high income.
